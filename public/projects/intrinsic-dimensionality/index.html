What is being approximated here?

Inside a small neighborhood around a selected point, we treat density as nearly constant and geometry as locally Euclidean; both are approximations.

Why logs?

Logs turn products into sums and ratios remove unknown scale constants, yielding a stable score equation in terms of distance ratios.

Why this works locally but can fail globally

Bigger k lowers variance yet can include nonlocal structure (curvature, boundaries, mixed scales), violating the derivation assumptions.

Reference: Levina, E. & Bickel, P. J. (2005), Maximum Likelihood Estimation of Intrinsic Dimension.
