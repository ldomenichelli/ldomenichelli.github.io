Move history

    • Base Azul uses 5 factory displays in a 2-player game and 4 tiles per factory.
    • On your turn, take all tiles of one color from a single factory or from the center.
    • The first player to take tiles from the center also takes the starting-player marker and adds it to the floor line.
    • Chosen tiles must go to exactly one pattern line or to the floor. A pattern line can hold only one color, and its wall row cannot already contain that color.
    • At the end of the round, each completed pattern line moves one tile to the wall, scores immediately, and sends the extras to the discard.
    • The game ends after the wall-tiling phase in which at least one wall row is complete. Bonus points come from completed rows, columns, and color sets.
    1. Click a color group in a factory or in the center to select your draft.
    2. The active player board highlights every legal pattern line and shows how many tiles would spill to the floor.
    3. Click a legal line to place there, or click the floor line if you want to dump everything.
    4. Invalid rows explain why they are blocked, so it stays clear whether the issue is color mismatch, a full line, or a wall conflict.
    5. Undo in Human vs Computer mode rewinds a full human/computer pair when that is the natural reversal.
    • Large pattern lines are tempting, but floor penalties often erase a flashy draft if the timing is wrong.
    • Watch the opponent's wall rows: denying a single color can strand a nearly completed pattern line for a full round.
    • Taking the first-player marker is strongest when you can absorb the penalty and exploit the next opening draft.
    • Rows matter for the end trigger, but columns and complete color sets are where big late-game swings come from.
