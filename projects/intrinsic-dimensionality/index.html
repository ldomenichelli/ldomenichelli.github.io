Formula + derivation passages

$$\hat m_k(x)=\left[\frac{1}{k-1}\sum_{j=1}^{k-1}\log\frac{T_k(x)}{T_j(x)}\right]^{-1},\quad \hat m=\frac{1}{n}\sum_{i=1}^{n}\hat m_k(x_i)$$
  1. Count neighbors in $S_x(t)$: $$N(t,x)=\sum_i \mathbf{1}\{X_i\in S_x(t)\}$$
  2. Assume local Poisson intensity: $$\lambda(t)=f(x)V(m)mt^{m-1}$$
  3. Build log-likelihood and solve for $m$.
  4. Switch from radius-$R$ form to practical $k$-NN form.

Warning: high noise / many outliers can inflate or destabilize estimates.
