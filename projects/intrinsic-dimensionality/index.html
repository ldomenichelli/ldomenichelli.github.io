Intrinsic Dimensionality · Levina–Bickel MLE

Estimating manifold dimension from local distance ratios

All estimator math below is shown in LaTeX, and the values are computed from the currently displayed neighborhood.

Local estimator

$$\hat{m}_k(x)=\left[\frac{1}{k-1}\sum_{j=1}^{k-1}\log\frac{T_k(x)}{T_j(x)}\right]^{-1}$$

Here $T_j(x)$ is the $j$-th nearest-neighbor distance from $x$. The point inspector computes every term in the sum and updates $\hat{m}_k(x)$ live.

$$\hat{m}_{\text{global}}(k)=\frac{1}{N}\sum_{i=1}^{N}\hat{m}_k(x_i)$$

The aggregate panel estimates a dataset-level dimension, and reports an interquartile spread to show instability.

Failure-mode controls

Use sliders to stress the assumptions behind local Poisson-like neighbor behavior.

  • Boundary clipping: clips point coordinates to $[\beta,1-\beta]^2$, increasing boundary bias.
  • Outliers: replaces a fraction of points with uniform noise in the ambient square.
  • Noise: adds isotropic Gaussian perturbations.

Interpret estimates as local summaries, not a single “true” number across all scales.
