Intrinsic Dimensionality · ESSa(d=1)

Expected Simplex Skewness (ESS)

ESS looks at centroid vectors in a local neighborhood: in higher intrinsic dimensions, random vector pairs are more often close to orthogonal, so average simplex area grows.

Interactive local neighborhood explorer

Neighborhoods are sampled uniformly from an n-dimensional unit ball, with optional isotropic Gaussian noise. ESS is computed in true dimension, visuals use a 2D projection.
