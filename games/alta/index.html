Move history

    • Alta uses the official 61-square board from the original W. D. Troyka package.
    • On each turn, place a new diagonal switch of your color or toggle one of your own switches to the opposite diagonal.
    • Blue connects top to bottom; Red connects left to right.
    • Winning connections may use switches of either color, not only your own.
    • You may not place a switch in the three spaces next to each enemy goal node.
    • If one move creates both winning connections, the moving player wins immediately.
    1. Click any legal empty square, then choose `/` or `\` inside that square.
    2. Click one of your own switches to toggle it to the opposite diagonal.
    3. In Human vs Computer mode, input locks while the AI is thinking.
    4. Undo in Human vs Computer mode rewinds a full pair of moves when possible.
    • Build threats in both circuits so a single enemy toggle cannot stop everything.
    • Central cells matter because they can pivot your path between diagonal and orthogonal control lines.
    • Enemy switches are still useful material: exploit them instead of treating them like dead blockers.
    • Toggling is strongest when it changes a quiet support switch into an immediate connection or block.
